What is Microsoft PowerPoint?

Microsoft PowerPoint is a popular presentation application developed by Microsoft. PowerPoint is part of the Microsoft Office suite containing other software like Excel, Word, and Access among others. Not only Windows, PowerPoint can also be run on Android, MacOS, iOS, and even on the web.

Today, PowerPoint is used anywhere from educational institutions to offices to convey messages and share ideas. Especially during the COVID-19 pandemic, teachers started teaching through PowerPoint since traditional blackboard teaching was not an option anymore.

Why are PowerPoint skills important?

Since PowerPoint has such a wide variety of uses, it’s only natural that it’s a much sought-after skill. According to a research by the Department for Digital, Culture, Media and Sport (DCMS) of the Government of UK, Microsoft PowerPoint is one of the most common productivity software skills requested by UK employers. It has 185,619 job adverts, a high advertised salary of £34,300 and a versatility of 0.98 (very high).

What is my experience with PowerPoint?

I’ve been using Microsoft PowerPoint since my school days, not just for work but for fun as well! I’ve always seen my father make presentations to teach his class. Back then, it was way ahead of time, and a much more interactive method of teaching.

I am a Guided Project Instructor at Coursera. I am also a Beta Tester at Coursera, having tested many courses before they’re officially launched. I have taken over 50 online courses in various subjects.

My experience as an online learner and teacher has given me some perspective on what to look for in an online course. I used my experience to evaluate each course in this list.

Best Courses Guides Methodology

I built this ranking following the now tried-and-tested methodology I used in previous rankings (you can find them all here). It involves a three-step process:

  1. Research: I started by leveraging Class Central’s database with over 80K+ online courses and 170K reviews. Then, I made a preliminary selection of 180+ Microsoft PowerPoint courses by rating, reviews, and bookmarks.

  3. Select: Well-made courses were picked if they presented valuable and engaging content and they have to fit in a set of criteria and be ranked accordingly: comprehensive curriculum, release date, affordability, ratings and enrollments.

The end result is a unique selection of courses that combines a decade of Class Central data and my own experience as an online learner to try to get the best of both worlds. So far, I’ve spent more than 10 hours building this list, and I’ll continue to update it.

1. Diseña presentaciones eficaces con Powerpoint (València)

My first pick for the best Microsoft PowerPoint online course is the free-to-audit Diseña presentaciones eficaces con Powerpoint, offered by Universitat Politècnica de València on edX.

So if you’re a beginner and looking for a one-stop course to learn PowerPoint, then think no more!

In the next module, you’ll be introduced to Powerpoint. You’ll learn about presentations in teaching, create your first presentation, improve your existing presentations, add elements such as SmartArt and tables, and work with files in PowerPoint.

Finally, in the last module, you learn to add content to your presentation. You’ll work on  PowerPoint design and PowerPoint views, add transitions and animations in PowerPoint, insert hyperlinks, apply slide show, and share presentations with others.

How You’ll Learn

This includes knowing your audience, knowing your purpose, structuring the body of your presentation, planning how you will start your presentation, planning how you will end your presentation, preparing your visual aids, anticipating the questions you may be asked, and practicing your presentation.

In the next week, you’ll learn the different types of communications styles. You’ll start off by understanding your personal professional presence and how to maximize it. You’ll learn about verbal and nonverbal communications and strategies to enhance your questioning and listening skills. You’ll also see how differences in culture can impact how you communicate.

After that, in the third week, you’ll learn how to create effective slides using PowerPoint. You’ll learn about the tools available in PowerPoint, how to structure your storyline, create storyboards, identify primary elements of slide design, display data and finalize your slide presentation.

Finally, in the fourth week, paying learners will build and deliver a presentation to your peers, and receive feedback from them. You’ll create a presentation of about 10 slides, with the help of the guidelines and industry best practices that have been discussed in this course.

How You’ll Learn

The course is broken down into 4 weeks. Each week involves about 1 hour of work. Concepts are taught through a combination of videos and readings.

For paying learners, there are a number of graded quizzes as well as peer-graded assignments throughout the course. All this will add to your final score in the course.
