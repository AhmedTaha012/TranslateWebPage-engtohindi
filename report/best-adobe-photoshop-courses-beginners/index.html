9 Best Adobe Photoshop CC Courses for Beginners to Take in 2023

This guide covers the best Adobe Photoshop courses for beginners. Get started now with one of the most popular graphic design software in the market.

Fabio Dantas May 30th, 2022
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Envelope
  • 6

Adobe Photoshop is ubiquitous and has become synonymous with graphic design. Even if you have never used Photoshop before, you probably have seen extraordinary digital art made with it. Reality is, this program is way more powerful than it used to be and it can be used not only for photography and web design but also for a variety of digital art forms, animated GIF, illustration, and much more.

Although, trying to find the best online course for learning Adobe Photoshop can be overwhelming with so many options; we have 1,100+ Adobe Photoshop courses on Class Central. Therefore, after 6 hours of research, I’ve created this guide with the best Adobe Photoshop CC online courses and tutorials, and ranked them using a well-defined methodology. If you are on a budget, 3 courses are completely free!

Course Workload In Brief
1. Photoshop for Beginners (Envato Tuts+) 3-4 hours Best for the time constrained to learn the essentials for free

9. Adobe Photoshop Mega Course-From Beginner to Super Designer (Udemy) 18 hours Fast paced and comprehensive covers basics and advanced techniques

What is Adobe Photoshop?

Photoshop is Adobe’s graphics editor for Windows and macOS. It’s the second most used graphic design software with 28% of market share, behind Adobe Illustrator with 42%. It’s quite impressive how it’s still so relevant after over 30 years in the market!

Besides being so popular among photo editing enthusiasts and professionals, Photoshop has become an important tool for digital artists and web designers as well, being fully integrated with the drawing tablets it makes the perfect combo from start to finish of any composition.

You can purchase Adobe Photoshop as a standalone app for a monthly subscription of about $10, or all Adobe apps for about $55 (20+ Creative Cloud apps, including XD, Photoshop, Illustrator, and After Effects) with a 7 day free trial. Adobe also offers a 50% discount for students and teachers.

Best Courses Guides Methodology

I built this ranking following the now tried-and-tested methodology used in previous Best Courses Guides (you can find them all here). It involves a three-step process:

  1. Research: I started by leveraging Class Central’s database with 70K+ online courses and 170K reviews. Then, I made a preliminary selection of 1,100+ Adobe Photoshop courses by rating, reviews, and bookmarks.
  2. Evaluate: I read through reviews on Class Central, Reddit, and course providers to understand what other learners thought about each course and combined it with my own experience as a learner.
  3. Select: Well-made courses were picked if they presented valuable and engaging content and they have to fit in a set of criteria and be ranked accordingly: comprehensive curriculum, selling price, release date, ratings and enrollments.

Course Ranking Statistics

  • 3 courses are Free and 6 are Paid.
  • 8 courses are in English, and 1 in Spanish with English subtitles.
  • Together, they account for 1,013,717 enrollments and over 10 million YouTube views.
  • The most featured provider is Udemy with 4 courses.

3. Master Photoshop in 30 Days (PHLEARN)

My #3 pick is for Master Photoshop in 30 Days by PHLEARN.

In this free course, you will take you from a complete beginner all the way to the point where you know how to retouch, do a composite, edit raw photos, remove objects, and work with text and graphics in 30 days. What You’ll Learn

In the first 10 days you will learn how to navigate Photoshop by opening, editing and saving files, how to use layers and masks, create selections, levels and curves, change any color with hue and saturation, transform tool and brush, and end this first part with crop and resize.

From day 11 to 20, you will learn how to add text, use liquify tool, edit non-destructively with Smart Objects, use blending modes, remove distractions, dodge and burn, create a photo collage, edit RAW, and color grade a photo.

In the last 10 days, you will learn how to change color, correct exposure, remove blemishes, use frequency retouching, sharpen a portrait, cut out a subject and hair with Pen tool, match light and color for composites, edit landscapes in camera RAW, and finally, swap a sky in a landscape.

7. Adobe Photoshop for Beginners (Domestika)

My seventh pick is for Adobe Photoshop for Beginners with Harrison Kuykendall, Creative Director & Retoucher.

In this series of courses you will learn tips and tricks for creating impactful digital work. Follow along as the expert teaches you how to become proficient in Photoshop, from the basics to creating an animated GIF.

By the end of this course, you’ll have all the skills and tools you need to transform images, whether you’re retouching portraits, editing beauty and fashion photography, or creating dynamic animations from still images.

Great course, very practical. The fact that each class is short makes it more dynamic and convenient. – samirnahas44, learner

What You’ll Learn

In the first course, get started by building a foundation of the software: navigation and basic tools, drop-down menus for editing images, and more.

For the second course, dive into the basics of color and light: how to adjust and modify the highlights and shadows of your image, including the different techniques for burning.

In the third course, go through the different methods for making selections and masks: learn the main techniques for cutting out elements using tools like Wand and Pen.

In the fourth course, learn the basic concepts of retouching: how to correct imperfections and see how to clean up skin, understand how to keep your retouching look natural and non-destructive, and how to use the Clone Stamp tool and export your retouched image.

For the fifth course, it’s time to focus on retouching still images. Learn the unique challenges and tools used to edit photos and discover Harrison’s secrets for getting the shape and perspective of still images just right.

Wrap up by learning about image sizing and manipulation. Learn to scale, skew, and distort your images with a range of tools. Take a look at two different warping tools and how you can use them to create animated GIFs.
