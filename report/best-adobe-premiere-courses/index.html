What is Adobe Premiere?

Adobe Premiere, better known as Adobe Premiere Pro, is an industry-leading video editing software that lets you create stunning videos fast. Whether from a professional camera or from your phone, you can edit all kinds of footage in Premiere Pro – upto 8K!

Premiere Pro has all the tools you need to import and edit, add effects and export footage. It also comes with professional templates and music tracks to make your storytelling unique. Premiere Pro also works with other apps and services, including Adobe Photoshop, After Effects and Frame.io. A 7-day free trial is available for Premiere Pro.

Versions of Adobe Premiere

The original version of Adobe Premiere was first launched in 1991, and its final version was released in 2002. A year later, in 2003, it was replaced by Adobe Premiere Pro, a rewritten version of Adobe Premiere. In short, Premiere Pro refers to versions released in 2003 and later, whereas Premiere refers to the earlier releases.

Adobe Premiere Pro Creative Cloud (CC) is the cloud-based version of Premiere Pro that works on monthly Creative Cloud plan subscription. It always gives you access to the latest versions of the software and even features collaborative cloud environments to work with a remote team.

Why are Adobe Premiere Skills Important?

The video editing market is well-established with plenty of professionals ready to tackle any project. According to Straits Research, the video editing market will reach $3.24 billion by 2030 and there definitely are opportunities even for newcomers!

At the moment, there are over 2,570 jobs posted for Adobe Premiere Specialist on Upwork and the average salary for this position is of USD 58K a year in the US according to Glassdoor.
