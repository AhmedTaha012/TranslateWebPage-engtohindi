What is SketchUp?

SketchUp is a 3D modeling and rendering software that has a wide range of drawing and design applications in the fields of architecture, interior design, product design, civil and mechanical engineering, video game development, and many more. It even allows you to place your models within Google Earth.

SketchUp was originally developed by @Last Software in 2000, which was later acquired by Google in 2006. A number of newer versions of the software came out before SketchUp was yet again acquired by its current owner, Trimble Navigation (now Trimble Inc.), in 2012.

You can try different versions of SketchUp according to your needs including a free, web-based one that comes with the essential features of the software.

Why are SketchUp Skills Important?

SketchUp is named the #1 architecture software program in the world by G2’s Grid Report for Architecture, Summer 2022. 

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for drafters was $60,290 in May 2021 while their employment is projected to grow 3% from 2021 to 2031, with about 18,900 openings projected each year, on average, over the decade.

The requirements for this job is complete high school education, CAD and SketchUp skills. Moreover, there are nearly 18,000 job openings for Drafters on Indeed.com. Clearly, having SketchUp in your skillset is a plus when it comes to employability.

What is My Experience with SketchUp?

I first came to know about SketchUp from the Guided Project, Product Design: Designing a modern table lamp using SketchUp. I took it back in 2020 and I made (as the name suggests) a 3D table lamp. Thinking about it now as a civil engineer, I can see SketchUp has quite some good applications in the construction and architecture domain as well.

Besides that, I am a Guided Project Instructor and a Beta Tester at Coursera, having tested many courses before they’re officially launched. I have taken over 50 online courses in various subjects.

My experience as an online learner and teacher has given me some perspective on what to look for in an online course. I used my experience to evaluate each course in this list.

Course Ranking Methodology

I built this ranking following the now tried-and-tested methodology I used in previous rankings (you can find them all here). It involves a three-step process:

  1. Research: I started by leveraging Class Central’s database with 100K online courses and 200K+ reviews. Then, I made a preliminary selection of 100+ SketchUp courses by rating, reviews, and bookmarks.
  2. Evaluate: I read through reviews on Class Central, Reddit, and course providers to understand what other learners thought about each course and combined it with my own experience as a learner.
  3. Select: Well-made courses were picked if they presented valuable and engaging content and they have to fit in a set of criteria and be ranked accordingly: comprehensive curriculum, release date, affordability, ratings and enrollments.

The end result is a unique selection of courses that combines a decade of Class Central data and my own experience as an online learner to try to get the best of both worlds. So far, I’ve spent more than 10 hours building this list, and I’ll continue to update it.

1. SketchUp 2021 Essential Training (LinkedIn Learning)

My first pick for the best SketchUp online course is SketchUp 2021 Essential Training on LinkedIn Learning.

Designed for beginners, this course will help you grasp the basics of SketchUp with just a couple of hours. You’ll get a foundational understanding of SketchUp, learn to use its drawing and design tools, and apply simple styles and animation. What’s more – you’ll also get enough practice opportunities throughout the course to test your understanding.

What You’ll Learn

In the first module, you’ll explore the SketchUp interface. You’ll choose a template, learn the basics for using a Windows and Mac interface, customize the toolbar for Mac, use the axis, standard camera views and zoom tools, move the camera view with Orbit and Pan tools, set a camera view and the Look Around tool, and walk around the model.

In the second module, you’ll manipulate objects. You’ll use the Select tool and the Move tool, create copies and an array, resize an object, rotate an object, flip and mirror an object, and learn about the 3D Warehouse and why polygons matter.

In the third module, you’ll draw in SketchUp. You’ll use the Line tool, Rectangle tool, Circle tool, Push/Pull into 3D, Tape Measure tool, Protractor tool, Undo and let go, Eraser tool, Dividing lines, and Offset tool.

In the fourth module, you’ll model from a floor plan image. You’ll import and prep, draw a floor plan, extrude walls, and add doorways and windows.

In the fifth module, you’ll learn about organization. You’ll make groups, make components, isolate a group or component, use Tags and Entity Info, and the Outliner window.

In the sixth module, you’ll learn about materials. You’ll apply material using a Mac, edit material using a Mac, apply material using Windows, edit material using Windows, apply material using 3D Warehouse, import and apply a custom texture, and purge material list.

Finally, in the seventh module, you’ll document and stylize. You’ll set shadows and fog, change the style, set and update scenes, check edge alignment, create a section cut, dimension, draw dashed lines, label, create 3D text, animate, and export in 2D and 3D.

How You’ll Learn

The course is broken down into 7 modules. Each module involves about 15 to 30 minutes of work. Concepts are taught through a combination of videos and practice exercises.

2. Learning SketchUp Free (LinkedIn Learning)

My second pick for the best SketchUp online course is Learning SketchUp Free on LinkedIn Learning.

SketchUp Free is the free, web-based version of the software that contains many of the features found in the paid versions such as SketchUp Pro. If you’re a beginner, this is a great opportunity for you if you want to try your hands at SketchUp and even learn some advanced techniques before moving to the paid versions if you want.

What You’ll Learn

In the first module, you’ll explore the SketchUp Free interface. You’ll learn about interface basics, opening files from Trimble Connect, inserting files, loading components via 3D Warehouse, navigating in SketchUp, and walking around in SketchUp.

You’ll also learn about standard camera views, display shadows and fog, creating multiple views using scenes, applying styles and modeling info window, and solve a challenge on scenes and styles.

In the second module, you’ll draw in SketchUp. You’ll understand the Line tool fundamentals, 3D drawing with the Line tool, using the Rectangle tool, creating rotated rectangles, pushing and pulling faces in 3D and using the Eraser tool, and solve a challenge on basic drawing.

In the third module, you’ll work on advanced drawing. You’ll learn about drawing curved and freehand shapes, creating circles and polygons, softening and smoothing edges, creating outlines with Offset, using the Follow Me tool and creating 3D text, and solve a challenge on advanced drawing.

In the fourth module, you’ll manipulate objects. You’ll explore selecting and moving objects, scaling and rotating objects, manipulating faces and edges and duplicating objects, and solve a challenge on drawing with duplication.

In the fifth module, you’ll learn about staying organized. You’ll understand grouping objects, creating components, component options, working with layers and entity info, and solve a challenge on components and groups.

In the sixth module, you’ll measure and label. You’ll use the Tape Measure tool and the Protractor tool, Creating labels with the Text tool, dimension, create cutaways using section planes, and solve a challenge on drawing with guides.

In the seventh module, you’ll learn about materials and textures. You’ll apply materials, create materials from textures, adjust texture placement, map curved objects, import images, and solve a challenge on adding materials.

Finally, in the eighth module, you’ll import and export. You’ll download SketchUp files, and export to 2D and 3D formats.

How You’ll Learn

The course is broken down into 8 modules. Each module involves about 15 to 40 minutes of work. Concepts are taught through a combination of videos and practice exercises.

3. SketchUp for Architecture (LinkedIn Learning)

My third pick for the best SketchUp online course is SketchUp for Architecture on LinkedIn Learning.

Are you an architect looking to create cool CAD models of buildings using SketchUp? Then this intermediate-level course is a must for you. Right from designing the site plan to adding walls, floors, roof and even furniture and appliances, this course will take you through the whole process of transforming simple drawings into 3D presentations.

What You’ll Learn

In the first module, you’ll be introduced to the course. You’ll understand what you should know before watching this course, and how to use the exercise files.

In the second module, you’ll set up SketchUp. You’ll learn about templates, toolbars, and model settings, moving around the model, understanding and creating components, saving and loading components, and what plugins are and where to find them.

In the third module, you’ll see how to work with SketchUp, not against it. You’ll learn to think like SketchUp, explore sweeps and revolves, inferencing and X-ray, groups, components and the Outliner window, and organize your model with layers and scenes.

In the fourth module, you’ll model the house. You’ll be introduced to modeling the house, and learn about importing 2D CAD drawings, adding the base, building the external shell, shaping the external shell slopes (Pro and Make methods), forming the openings and high-level window openings, add internal walls, low-level doors and window, and high-level windows (front and side face), and adding the roof.

In the fifth module, you’ll work with materials. You’ll be introduced to working with materials, the Materials Editor (Windows and Mac interfaces), apply materials to objects, import images as textures, create textures from scratch (Windows and Mac), add materials to walls, roofs, doors and windows, and modify the material placement.

In the sixth module, you’ll explore landscaping and external features. You’ll be introduced to landscaping, and add the hardstanding, walls, wall coping, water feature, stepping-stones, path edge and infill, grass, grass border and external planting.

In the seventh module, you’ll create interior furnishings. You’ll be introduced to modeling the interior fixtures, add the chimney stack, create the fire pit and the island unit, build the low units, high units and left-side kitchen units, and add the pelmet and base trim.

You’ll also add the work surface, drop in the sink top, add the cooker and hood, add the fridge, decorate the west-wing interior, and load the furniture and finishes.

In the eighth module, you’ll style the model. You’ll be introduced to styling the model, use the style window, create and save a new style, add a watermark, and use styles for section cuts.

In the ninth module, you’ll learn about scenes. You’ll be introduced to scenes, the scene window, and create the elevation scenes, plans and sections, and Perspective views with shadows.

Finally, you’ll geolocate the model. You’ll learn about the importance of accurately locating the model, mapping the shadows to the views, and animating a simple shadow study.

How You’ll Learn

The course is broken down into 10 modules. Each module involves about 25 minutes to 1 hour of work. Concepts are taught through a combination of videos and practice exercises.
