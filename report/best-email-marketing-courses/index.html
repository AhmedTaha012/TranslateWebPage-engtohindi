What is Email Marketing?

Email marketing is the process of sending commercial emails to a group of people to build business relationships. These people are your existing or potential customers who can help broaden your brand.

Emails can be in the form of advertisements, business requests or donations, with the objective of building loyalty, trust and brand awareness.

Why are Email Marketing skills important?

Email marketing has a return of investment (ROI) of $36 for every $1 spent, which means that if you spend $1, you’ll get $35 back. In 2019, there were 3.9 billion email users in the world. This number is expected to grow to 4.48 billion by 2024. That’s half the world’s population!

It has been seen that 72% of US consumers prefer email marketing over other types of promotion. A recent study by Litmus shows that consumers project email marketing to be the most relevant marketing channel in the next 10 years.

According to the US Burning Glass Labor Insight Report, 86% of business leaders report that digital commerce will be the most important route to growth. There are 218,000 U.S. job openings in this growing field, with a median entry-level salary of $51,000.

Besides that, I am a Guided Project Instructor and a Beta Tester at Coursera, having tested many courses before they’re officially launched. I have taken over 50 online courses in various subjects.

My experience as an online learner and teacher has given me some perspective on what to look for in an online course. I used my experience to evaluate each course in this list.

Course Ranking Methodology

I built this ranking following the now tried-and-tested methodology I used in previous rankings (you can find them all here). It involves a three-step process:

  1. Research: I started by leveraging Class Central’s database with 100K online courses and 200K+ reviews. Then, I made a preliminary selection of 100+ email marketing courses by rating, reviews, and bookmarks.
  2. Evaluate: I read through reviews on Class Central, Reddit, and course providers to understand what other learners thought about each course and combined it with my own experience as a learner.
  3. Select: Well-made courses were picked if they presented valuable and engaging content and they have to fit in a set of criteria and be ranked accordingly: comprehensive curriculum, release date, affordability, ratings and enrollments.

The end result is a unique selection of courses that combines a decade of Class Central data and my own experience as an online learner to try to get the best of both worlds. So far, I’ve spent more than 12 hours building this list, and I’ll continue to update it.

1. Think Outside the Inbox: Email Marketing (Google)

My first pick for the best email marketing online course is the free-to-audit Think Outside the Inbox: Email Marketing, offered by Google on Coursera.

Being the tech giant that Google is, there’s no doubt that this course will easily make it to the first spot on this list. A 2021 report shows that 75% of Google Career Certificate graduates in the US report see a positive impact on their career within six months.

This means taking this course will prepare you for a new career in the high-growth fields of email marketing. You’ll learn from subject-matter experts at Google, get hands-on experience, and will be equipped to apply for entry-level jobs.

Excellent course. I learnt a lot from it and my teacher was excellent! Thank you so much and I hope I will be able to apply this information once I get a job! – Gaurav B, Coursera learner

What You’ll Learn

In the first week, you’ll be introduced to email marketing. You’ll learn what email marketing is, its importance, and how to meet business goals. You’ll also use the PESTLE and SWOT frameworks to set SMART email marketing goals, and apply the best practices in email marketing.

In the next week, you’ll take a look at the most common types of email marketing. You’ll learn about acquisition emails and welcome emails, newsletters and promotional emails, and retention emails. You’ll also craft email copy, write subject lines, and create preview text.

After that, in the third week, you’ll learn about useful tools for email marketing. You’ll create and segment email lists, write engaging emails, work with email marketing tools like HubSpot and Mailchimp, and create an email marketing campaign report.

Finally, in the last week, you’ll conduct a mock email marketing campaign. You’ll apply all that you’ve learned during this course to complete the campaign. You’ll set SMART goals, segment your email list and complete an email series, and end the course by analyzing and presenting the results.

How You’ll Learn

The course is broken down into 5 weeks. Each week involves about 4 to 7 hours of work. Concepts are taught through a combination of videos, readings and practice quizzes.

Other than practice quizzes, there are other practice exercises in the form of plugins. These are interactive activities embedded separately within the course.

All in all, there’s a pretty good amount of practice opportunities in this course. Paid learners can take the graded quizzes and earn a certificate upon completion.

2. Email Marketing Course: Get Certified in Email Marketing (HubSpot Academy)

My second pick for the best email marketing online course is the free Email Marketing Course: Get Certified in Email Marketing, on HubSpot Academy.

Emails need to be human, helpful and holistic – this is what the instructor says at the beginning of the course. Indeed, if you want your company to stand out, it’s important not only to send emails to your clients, but to actually make them look sensible and real.

HubSpot is a leading developer and marketer of inbound marketing tools. Learning from these experts is a great way to get started with email marketing, and their certification adds extra credibility to your portfolio as well. What’s more: this certificate is completely free!

What You’ll Learn

You’ll begin the course by learning how to build an effective email marketing strategy. You’ll create a contact management and segmentation strategy so that you can send contextual emails and create engaging conversions.

Sending the right email gives you a better chance of converting your leads into customers. That’s why, you’ll take a look at the key components of how to send the right email to the right person at the time. You’ll also learn how to identify trends in your email deliverability metrics.

Next, you’ll understand why content and design are equally important for your marketing emails, and start designing emails that provide value. You’ll also learn why analyzing your emails is important and the steps you need to take to perform this analysis.

Before sending an email, it’s important to run tests to update those emails. That’s why you’ll learn what types of tests you can run on your marketing emails. Finally, you’ll hear from industry experts on how to develop relationships through lead nurturing. You’ll learn about the foundations of lead nurturing and how to implement them.

How You’ll Learn

The course is broken down into 9 modules. Each module involves about 15 to 30 hours of work. Concepts are taught through video lectures only.

Throughout the videos, you’ll not only learn about email marketing, but will also hear from industry experts, including HubSpot professionals. The course is completely free and all learners will get a certificate upon completion.

5. The ClickMinded Email Marketing Course (ClickMinded)

My fifth pick for the best email marketing online course is The ClickMinded Email Marketing Course, offered by ClickMinded.

What You’ll Learn

In the first module, you’ll learn why email marketing is different from any other channel, the three types of emails and two methods of distribution, and the three fundamental principles of email marketing.

In the next module, you’ll learn how to build a “lifecycle” strategy, borrow tactics from big companies,  “win back” users who have stopped interacting with your emails, understand “list hygiene”, and broadcast email to your entire list without ending up in spam.

In the third module, you’ll learn how to get the entire picture for each of your email subscribers, the 4 types of data: implicit data, explicit data, user personas, and engagement, and the right way to use tags and custom fields.

You’ll also take a look at 10 powerful segmentation strategies, implement segmentation, and understand how to use “decision-based workflows” to automatically segment and email subscribers based on the collected data.

In the next module, you’ll learn how to create a world-class email campaign, create a 3-step framework to overcome the major challenges, learn effective copywriting tactics to make your emails stand out, and use a step-by-step process to send error-free email campaigns.

After that, in the fifth module, you’ll get step-by-step walkthroughs on how to set up 8 powerful automated campaigns from scratch, and build a system that manages all of your campaigns and prevents conflicts between them.

Moving one, in the next module, you’ll explore the 8 metrics to measure and track before you optimize your campaigns, discover the three levers you can use to optimize any metric, and make sure your emails reach your subscribers’ inbox.
