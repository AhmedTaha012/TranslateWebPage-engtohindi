In previous Class Central articles, we compiled courses to help you prepare for your Google Cloud certification and your Microsoft Azure certification. This time, we continue our research by focusing on the current market leader: Amazon Web Services (AWS).

AWS is a cloud computing platform with pay-as-you-go APIs for a wide variety of uses. It comprises over 200 services for computing, storage, networking, database, analytics, and machine learning. With its 32% market share, AWS leads the cloud market, which has reached $63.1B in 2022.

AWS Certifications

AWS offers 13 certification programs, which are broken down into four categories:

  • Foundational — these are for complete beginners.
  • Associate — these build on your foundational cloud knowledge.
  • Professional — these aim to get you job-ready for advanced positions.
  • Specialty — these focus on specific domains, such as machine learning.

To earn a certification, learners have to pass a certification exam. These can be taken online or in a testing center. The foundational certification exam last 90 minutes and costs $100, while most professional and specialty certifications last 180 minutes and cost $300. The exams entail 65–75 multiple-choice questions.
