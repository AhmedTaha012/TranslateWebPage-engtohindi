What is Adobe After Effects?

Adobe After Effects is an industry-standard software for motion graphics design, video compositing and animation developed by Adobe Systems. Artists and post-production professionals use After Effects to create high-quality work for TV, fim, video and the web.

Using After Effects, you can animate texts and shapes to design title sequences and motion graphics, or even perform complex tasks like 3D rendering. This visual effects tool has got everything you need to bring text and graphics to life.

Like other Adobe tools, After Effects works seamlessly with other Adobe software like Photoshop, Illustrator, XD, Character Animator and Animate. What’s more – in 2019, Adobe After Effects even won an Academy Award for its scientific and technical achievements!

Why are Adobe After Effects Skills Important?

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for special effects artists and animators was $78,790 in May 2021. Their employment is projected to grow 5 percent from 2021 to 2031, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Moreover, on average, about 6,700 openings are projected each year over the decade. You can find more information on the employment and wages of special effects artists and animators here.

Besides that, according to CII, the design industry in India was worth almost Rs. 19K crore in 2020, which increased even more in the following year. For the UK, the average salary of full-time graphic artists is around £35K per year. Clearly, there are increasing opportunities in this market with growth across the globe.

1. Learn Adobe After Effects (Gareth David Studio)

My first pick for the best Adobe After Effects online course is the free course Learn Adobe After Effects, offered by Gareth David Studio on YouTube.

This free course is made for beginner designers. Whether you use the latest version of Adobe After Effects or an older one, since this course teaches you the basics of the software, you should be able to follow along simply by downloading the practice files. In this course, you’ll learn what After Effects is, be introduced to its interface, and learn about layers and the Timeline panel, compositions, the Project Panel ‘Links panel’, animation principles, keyframes, simple video editing, simple animation, easing, and time stretching.

Next, you’ll learn about precomposing, importing files, creating shapes, pen tool and paths, fill and stroke effects, animating along a path, effects and presets, text animation presets, transition presets, color effects, adjustment layers, blending modes, masking, and animating a shape layer mask.

After that, you’ll explore track mattes, null objects and parenting, exporting and handy tips, plan and design for a video presentation, prepare and set up a document, animate type with an image, animate a bouncing pin, a simple transition and a British Flag sequence, and synchronize to music.

Finally, you’ll edit a video sequence, duplicate and edit an animation sequence, build a complex layout and animate it, animate a shape layer mask, duplicate and edit sequences, animate still images, create color enhancing footage, create a 2D scene, and animate a simple 2D scene.

8. After Effects CC 2022 Essential Training (LinkedIn Learning)

My eighth pick for the best Adobe After Effects online course is After Effects CC 2022 Essential Training on LinkedIn Learning.

Learn the essentials of Adobe After Effects quickly and easily, and watch your creativity come alive on screen. From shape layer tools and dynamic type animators to visual effect workflows and 3D compositing workspace, you’ll learn all the techniques you need in order to create better animations in After Effects. A 1-month free trial is available with this course.

What You’ll Learn

The first module is about working with layers and shapes. You’ll get an overview of the shape layer, and learn about transformation levels, shape layers vs. masks, repeating elements, path operators, precomposing and nesting, collapse transformations and blending modes.

The second module is about animating in After Effects. You’ll explore keyframe interpolations, add and adjust keys, use the Graph Editor for speed and value, alter speeds with time remapping, understand anchor point fundamentals and parenting, use expressions to loop animation, and work with the Essential Graphics panel and essential properties.

The third module is about creating transparency for compositing. You’ll learn about creating and animating masks, using track mattes, using the Roto Brush tool, Mocha AE crash course, and keylight.

The fourth module is about working with type. You’ll learn about activating Adobe fonts, using the type tool, and using text animators.

The fifth module is about working with effects. You’ll learn how to work with effects, be introduced to the Puppet tool, limit effects with mask references, and use Content-Aware Fill.

The sixth module is about basic After Effects 3D. You’ll take a look at the introductory concepts to 3D in After Effects, 3D navigation, cameras and lights, and 3D layer properties.

The seventh module is about 3D geometry. You’ll be introduced to Cinema4D renderer, create extruded 3D text and shapes, get an overview of Cinema4D Lite overview, and use Cineware.

The eighth module is about working with trackers. You’ll understand point tracking, Warp Stabilizer, Mocha AE planar tracking, and camera tracking.

The ninth and final module is about rendering. You’ll learn how to export to disk using the Render Queue, and render with Media Encoder.

How You’ll Learn

The course is broken down into 9 modules. Each module involves about 15 to 50 minutes of work. Concepts are taught through a combination of videos, practice exercises and quizzes.
