What is Negotiation?

A negotiation is a tactical discussion to settle differences by reaching a resolution that all the parties find acceptable. Negotiations may take place between buyers and sellers, employers and employees or a country and another. A negotiation may take as little as a few minutes to as long as months or years, depending on the complexity of the matter at stake.

In a negotiation, one of the parties puts its position forward while the other will either accept the terms presented or counter with its own position and the proceeding continues until both parties agree on a compromise. According to Forbes, to negotiate effectively, you must understand your own worth, and have an action plan in place as you move forward with your requests.

Negotiation is the art of looking past conflicts to reach a common ground with your opposing parties. An ideal negotiation is one in which all the opposing parties stand to gain in the final proposal. The skills and tactics for a negotiation to be successful are, I believe, some of the most essential ones we need to be armed with.

1. Successful Negotiation: Essential Strategies and Skills (University of Michigan)

My first pick for the best Negotiation course is Successful Negotiation: Essential Strategies and Skills by the University of Michigan.

This free-to-audit course will help you gain an advantage during negotiations as well as identify the possible traps hidden in them. The practical side of each topic has been demonstrated well by the experienced professor George Siedel.

If you want to know more about the course, you can also check out my in-depth review here. This course has also been featured on 250 Top FREE Coursera Courses of All Time and The 250 Most Popular Online Courses of All Time.

What You’ll Learn

This course walks you through the various psychological tools you can use and traps that you should avoid during a negotiation like escalation, anchoring, and so on. You learn about alternative dispute resolution (ADR), cross-cultural negotiations, agents, deal-making mindset vs implementation mindset, big picture focus, and much more.

How You’ll Learn

The course spans over a period of 7 weeks and has a workload of 75 minutes per week. There are ungraded assignments given along with each module. The questions for assessment mostly test your practical knowledge. Once you pass the final exam with a grade of 80% or above, you will receive the certificate of completion.

2. Introduction to Negotiation: A Strategic Playbook for Becoming a Principled and Persuasive Negotiator (Yale University)

If you need a framework to analyze and shape negotiations, Introduction to Negotiation: A Strategic Playbook for Becoming a Principled and Persuasive Negotiator is for you.

This free-to-audit course allows you to penetrate within the conflicts to discover the underlying interests of the opposing party. After the completion of this course, you will be able to predict, interpret and shape the behavior of the opposite side.

This course has also been featured on 250 Top FREE Coursera Courses of All Time and The 250 Most Popular Online Courses of All Time.

What You’ll Learn

This course focuses on the theory and practical application of negotiation. The first half covers the theory of the “pie”, how to determine your share of the deal and its application. It also delves into case studies to help analyze the negotiation process and include various short lessons. In the second half, students are introduced to Professor Linda Babcock, a renowned expert on negotiation who specializes in the role of gender differences in negotiation. She covers the value of asking, preparation, and how to ask. There is also one module led by Herb Cohen, a corporate and government negotiation expert, who teaches that almost everything is negotiable. After that, the course focuses on experienced deal-making with Canadian businessman and philanthropist John McCall MacBain. Finally, the course concludes with some more additional reading materials.

How You’ll Learn

You will immerse yourself in the videos in each of the 9 weeks of the course duration. You will have opportunities to negotiate with other students in the course, given different case studies that are based on common situations. You will also receive feedback based on your performance which will help you understand what you did wrong and what could have been done better. There are multiple quizzes you can take to test your knowledge and numerous reading materials have also been supplied. You will receive a certificate upon the completion of the course.

If you are looking for a course to dive into the science of negotiation, this is the one for you: Chris Voss Teaches The Art Of Negotiation. As an FBI hostage negotiator, Chris Voss persuaded terrorists, bank robbers, and kidnappers to see things his way. And through his MasterClass, he will be imparting his expert knowledge in this field.

The course focuses on the collaborative aspect of a negotiation along with using tactical empathy and trust-based influence to your advantage. Bargaining, body language, speech patterns, neuroscience of bending people’s reality are all various topics that the course covers. You will be able to strengthen your communication skills, get game-changing insights into human nature, and understand more of what you want out of life.

How You’ll Learn

There are 18 lessons spanning a time period of 3 hours in total. You watch each of the video lessons to gain a better understanding of the topics that are covered. Upon finishing the course, you receive an email confirming your course completion.

4. Negotiation Skills (LinkedIn Learning) 

This short course in Negotiation Skills, taught by expert Chris Croft, is designed for beginners and it’s a great course to understand how to handle daily-life negotiations. The course focuses on providing practical skills that can be applied in various settings, such as business, sales, and personal situations. It’s of short duration, making it suitable for individuals who want to quickly learn the essentials of negotiation.

What You’ll Learn

This course covers the basics of negotiation, including planning, making opening offers, using specific tactics, achieving win-win outcomes, and closing deals.

You start this course by understanding how to decide whether to negotiate or not. Then you move onto planning your negotiation. From there, you get a hang of opening offers, diverse negotiation tactics and trading techniques. The last chapter focuses on the dos and don’ts while carrying out a negotiation.

How You’ll Learn

This course is divided into 5 chapters and 33 videos. With each chapter you are done with, you attempt a quiz to analyze your knowledge. A project file is also included to help you apply your learnings. You only need to spend 2-3 hours to complete the course, upon which you will receive a certificate.

Becoming a positive influence in your organization requires strong conflict resolution and negotiation skills. What You’ll Learn

You’ll start off by learning how to elaborate on resolving conflicts and negotiate agreements in the context of the present-day workplace. Then, you will learn the theories of conflict and from there, you go through the pathways to resolution. You’ll also learn how to sharpen and wield the power of your skill sets. After that, let’s learn the different stages and types of negotiations. Finally, you will learn about negotiations across cultural divisions and how to cope with complexity during negotiations.

How You’ll Learn

Each week’s content includes 4-6 videos along with a quiz at the end. The quizzes lean more on applying your knowledge for each of the problems. The recommended readings for the course are detailed and well-explained. You receive a certificate upon the completion of the course.

6. Negotiation Foundations (LinkedIn Learning)

This short course, Negotiation Foundations, is designed to help you learn the tactics that can help you succeed in every negotiation. It also includes tips, worksheets and practice opportunities to help develop your skills.

What You’ll Learn

The course covers the basics of negotiation, including when it is necessary and how to plan it out. It also covers research and preparation, including identifying priorities and designing options. 7. Negotiations (University of Pennsylvania) 

This free-to-audit course, Negotiations by the University of Pennsylvania, will help you learn the dynamics of negotiations to help you craft a successful strategy and manage potential conflict while building trust.

What You’ll Learn

The course starts off by describing the basic framework of every negotiation. From there, it moves onto the fundamentals like the “Zone of Agreement” and bargaining positions. After that, the course goes into the major topic of negotiation dynamics through which you learn about power dynamics, time pressure, detecting and countering deception, managing teams in negotiation, and more. The last module helps you set the right negotiator mindset by letting you understand the impact of emotions in a negotiation.

How You’ll Learn

The course takes you through 4 modules that span over 70-85 minutes each which is divided between 6-9 videos. There is a practice exercise after each module. The tutor’s teaching style makes it easy to grasp the concepts well. Additional reading materials are also provided. You receive a certificate upon the completion of the course.

Culture, consciously or not, influences a huge part of who we are and so it seems natural that it may lead to conflicts from homes, workspace, communities and all the way across nations. What You’ll Learn

The course starts off by illustrating the influence of cultures, moving on to the power and impact it has over negotiations and ends by demonstrating examples of different negotiation styles across diverse cultures.

How You’ll Learn

The course has 4 modules and there are around 6-7 video lessons per module. Each module contains practice quizzes and a graded quiz. To earn the certificate, you need to pass all the graded quizzes with a score of 80% or higher.

9. High Performance Collaboration: Leadership, Teamwork, and Negotiation (Northwestern University)

Efficient collaboration is a result of good leadership, teamwork and negotiation. Through this free-to-audit course, you will be able to learn to develop and expand your leadership skills, design teams for collaboration, and craft well-planned negotiation strategies. If you want to know how to negotiate as a leader, High Performance Collaboration: Leadership, Teamwork, and Negotiation is for you!

What You’ll Learn

The first module focuses on leadership and you will go through topics like the different leadership styles, motivation, the how of happiness, decision making along with emotional, organizational and cultural intelligence.

The second module centers around teamwork and will take you through goal-setting, diversity, roles, rules, optimizing conflict, virtual teams, creativity and innovation.

The third module helps you learn how to negotiate in a collaborative fashion through your BATNA (Best Alternative To A Negotiated Agreement), anchoring, art of concessions, value creation, interests and power.

How You’ll Learn

The course is divided into 3 modules, one for a week. Each module contains 1-hour long videos and the total estimated workload for the course is 14 hours. Adequate reading materials and up to 3 practice exercises are also supplied with each module. By finishing all the lectures and graded activities, you will receive a certificate of completion.

10. Fundamentals of Negotiation, with Goldman Sachs 10,000 Women

Fundamentals of Negotiation, with Goldman Sachs 10,000 Women, a free-to-audit course, will make you reconsider some common assumptions and preconceptions about negotiation, identify your preferred style and compare it with other styles of negotiation. It will equip you with the tools needed to negotiate confidently as you pursue opportunities for business growth.

What You’ll Learn

Through this course, you will understand the role of negotiation in business growth and your bargaining style opposed to others. You will be able to prepare well for a negotiation by defining the interests and positions involved along with the leverage for your negotiation. Using data to support your objectives, you will set your goals, expectations and bottom line to create a preparation plan. You will then understand the emotional aspects involved and learn to engage with others in the process. You will be able to identify the zone of possible agreement, listen effectively and learn how to shift leverage. You will also learn how to negotiate depending on the context.

How You’ll Learn

There are 5 lessons in the course. In each lesson you will have utmost 2 videos along with reading materials and interactive exercises which will help you learn the theory by applying and thinking them through. You will receive a certificate of completion after you pass the graded assignment given in Lesson 5 with a score of 75% or higher.
