What is Blender?

Blender is a free and open source 3D software. From modeling, rigging, animation, simulation and rendering to compositing, motion tracking, and even video editing and game creation, Blender supports the entire 3D pipeline.

Blender can run on Linux, Windows, and Mac computers, and lets the public make small and large changes to the code base, making it even more versatile and powerful.

Why are Blender Skills Important?

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for special effects artists and animators was $78,790 in May 2021. Their employment is projected to grow 5% from 2021 to 2031, with about 6,700 openings projected each year, on average, over the decade.

There are currently hundreds of job openings requiring Blender skills in companies such as Tesla, Nike, Apple, Activision Blizzard, EA (Electronic Arts) and others. You can check these positions as they are constantly updated on job searching websites: Indeed, LinkedIn and Glassdoor, just to mention a few. 

What is My Experience with Blender?

I have used Blender on my computer to edit videos. I’m still new to the software and learning what all things it can do. As someone interested in cartoons and animation, Blender is definitely on my bucket list.

Besides that, I am a Guided Project Instructor and a Beta Tester at Coursera, having tested many courses before they’re officially launched. I have taken over 50 online courses in various subjects.

My experience as an online learner and teacher has given me some perspective on what to look for in an online course. I used my experience to evaluate each course in this list.

Course Ranking Methodology

I built this ranking following the now tried-and-tested methodology I used in previous rankings (you can find them all here). It involves a three-step process:

  1. Research: I started by leveraging Class Central’s database with 100K online courses and 200K+ reviews. Then, I made a preliminary selection of 200 Blender courses by rating, reviews, and bookmarks.
  2. Evaluate: I read through reviews on Class Central, Reddit, and course providers to understand what other learners thought about each course and combined it with my own experience as a learner.
  3. Select: Well-made courses were picked if they presented valuable and engaging content and they have to fit in a set of criteria and be ranked accordingly: comprehensive curriculum, release date, affordability, ratings and enrollments.

The end result is a unique selection of courses that combines a decade of Class Central data and my own experience as an online learner to try to get the best of both worlds. So far, I’ve spent more than 11 hours building this list, and I’ll continue to update it.

2. Creating 3D environments in Blender (Udemy)

My second pick for the best Blender online course is Creating 3D environments in Blender on Udemy.

This extensive course is for anyone looking to build beautiful 3D environments in Blender. Whether you’re just starting out with Blender or already experienced in it, this course will teach you everything from scratch. With step-by-step guidance, you’ll become proficient in using Blender to create amazing 3D environments in no time.

What You’ll Learn

In the first chapter, you’ll get an introduction to the course, explanation about workflow and organization, motivation and inspiration, and course related questions.

In the second chapter, you’ll learn Blender fundamentals. You’ll take a look at its interface and the most used tools, and build a simple table/chair scene and a dice scene

In the third chapter, you’ll make grass strands. You’ll pack the strands and build a simple grass scene, use proxy models and model flowers, and animate the grass.

In the fourth chapter, you’ll create trees. You’ll create branches, add twigs, and upgrade the grass template.

In the fifth chapter, you’ll use the grass template and trees and work on the ruin structure. You’ll blend brick and plaster material, and model bricks.

In the sixth module, you’ll blend the rocks to make a river scene. You’ll shape the terrain, scatter the river with rocks, and animate the river.

In the seventh module, you’ll blend a castle with the terrain. You’ll build a sky with volumetric clouds and use the Nishita skylight to blend the sky to create a sunset.

In the eighth module, you’ll use grass, trees and paths to create a mountain path. You’ll fill the scene with ruin structures, and blend the sky with mountains and environmental scenes.

Finally, in the ninth module, you’ll explore the complete workflow to model a medieval street scene.

How You’ll Learn

The course is broken down into 14 sections. Each section involves about 2 to 8 hours of work. Concepts are taught through video lectures only. Downloadable resources are also provided.

4. Master Architectural Visualization – Blender 3x – Design (Udemy)

My fourth pick for the best Blender online course is Master Architectural Visualization – Blender 3x – Design on Udemy.

Even if you have no experience in 3D modeling, this course will help you master architectural visualization in Blender. This course will teach you how to successfully design and model stunning interior scenes in 3D in Blender step by step. You’ll start from 2D house layout and move all the way up to 3D modeling of rooms and lighting.

What You’ll Learn

In this course, you’ll learn about orthographic camera setup, freestyle line render, edge loops, separating objects, join command, freestyle edge mark, line thickness, dissolve edges, cursor to selected, selection to cursor, freestyle collections, and aligning objects.

Then, you’ll explore linking object data, extrusion, measureit add-on, dimensioning the floor plan, linked duplicate, mirror command, making single user, beveling vertex, limited dissolve, spin tool, increment snapping, array modifier, normals, knife tool, screw modifier, and bevel modifiers.

Next, you’ll take a look at aligning objects, symmetrize, link modifiers, subdivision modifier, Boolean modifier, mean crease, Bezier curve, parenting, collection manager, proportional editing, shrinkwrap modifier, invert selection, vertex groups, and matcap shading.

Finally, you’ll understand extruding regions, bridge, path curve, applying modifiers in order, vertex groups, copying to selected, rotation snapping, and spin modifier.

How You’ll Learn

The course is broken down into 10 sections. Each section involves about 30 minutes to 4.5 hours of work. Concepts are taught through a combination of videos, readings and quizzes. Downloadable resources are also provided.

8. Mantaflow Fluid Simulation Guide in Blender (Udemy)

My eighth pick for the best Blender online course is Mantaflow Fluid Simulation Guide in Blender on Udemy.

Mantaflow is an open-source fluid simulation framework. In other words, it is used to create realistic fluid flows in Blender. This course covers the basics of fluid simulation and applies that to simulate different kinds of motions of water under different situations. By the end of this course, you’ll see your water animations come to life.

What You’ll Learn

First, you’ll learn the basics of Blender, how it works, go through the domain and explore how every single setting and value affects the simulation.

Then, you’ll understand the different types of objects that can add to your simulation such as flow objects and obstacles guides, creating realistic water material, and ocean modifier.

Finally, you’ll learn about the rigid body simulation and how you can use it to interact with the fluid simulation, and how to create a realistic waterfall using Mantaflow.

How You’ll Learn

The course is broken down into 6 sections. Each section involves about 30 minutes to 1 hour of work. Concepts are taught through a combination of videos, readings and quizzes. Downloadable resources are also provided.
